The goal of this website is to turn scientific data on plastics and microplastics into 
accessible explanations, so public understands where plastics and microplastics come from, 
how they accumulate in the environment and human body, and how everyday choices can meaningfully reduce 
their contribution to global platic pollution and exposure. 

Grounded in behaviour change research, the site aims to shift attitudes, reshape social norms around 
single‑use plastics and synthetic clothing, and strengthen people’s sense of control by 
offering feasible actions at different effort and cost levels, all backed by academic and official sources.

Targeting population in upper-middle and high-income countries can result in better policies, activist movements, 
world-spread articles, better regulations and volunteering actions around plastic pollution on meso- and macro-levels.
Utilizing official data and academic sources can strengthen the main argument of the website, help with designing effective
action guides for community-based interventions, and prevent misinformation by introducing transparency into the design. 

The purpose of this website, therefore, is to contribute to bigger challenge of climate change 
and inform people about potential health risks of plastic pollution on the rise of microplastic research.
