The Ocean Pays First

You might picture plastic pollution as visible bottles and bags floating at the surface. That's just the beginning.

Under relentless UV light and friction, every piece of plastic slowly fractures — shedding particles that sink, disperse, and enter the food chain invisibly.

It's Already Inside You

Microplastics have been detected in human tissue in peer-reviewed studies. Not traces — measurable concentrations.

Open a card for a plain-language note. Detection does not by itself prove disease risk.

🧠 Brain 2024

Recent reports describe microplastic-like particles in autopsy and surgical samples; debate continues over contamination control during analysis.

🫁 Lungs 2022

Inhaled fibres can lodge deep in lung tissue; occupational and urban air studies show persistent synthetic particles.

❤️ Heart 2023

Cardiovascular tissues have been examined for polymer-associated pigments and particles; clinical significance is an active research area.

🩸 Blood 2022

Circulating fragments imply systemic transport; quantification methods and particle identities still vary between labs.

🫀 Liver 2021

The liver filters bloodborne material; studies report polymer fragments and additives in post-mortem and biopsy contexts.

🤱 Placenta 2020

Particles near the maternal–fetal interface raise questions about developmental exposure; evidence is evolving and method-sensitive.

What the Science Says

Causality is still being established — no control group of unexposed humans exists. But preliminary evidence is consistent across independent research groups.

Inflammation & oxidative stress Strong preliminary evidence
Context

Cell and animal models often show immune activation when exposed to relevant doses; human translation is incomplete.

Endocrine disruption Moderate evidence
Context

Plastics carry plasticisers and other additives that can interfere with hormone signalling in controlled experiments.

Cellular damage & bioaccumulation Moderate–strong evidence
Context

Particles below certain sizes can be internalised by cells; persistence and dose–response in people remain open questions.

Cardiovascular disease link Emerging evidence (2024)
Context

Some observational work links detected particles with plaque characteristics; confounding and causation are not settled.
