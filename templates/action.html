Action starts with knowledge

The threat of microplastics is concerning. But what can you do?

Routes of Exposure

Microplastics enter the body through every route imaginable: what you drink, eat, breathe, and wear.

Water

81%

of tap water samples worldwide contain microplastic fibres

Kosuth et al. (2018)

Food

88%

of tested protein products (seafood, meat, plant-based) contain microplastics

Milne et al. (2024)

Clothing

500-700K

microplastic particles are released from a single 6 kg domestic wash

Napper & Thompson (2016)

Air

~71,000

estimated total of microplastics inhaled daily by a human adult indoors.

Yakovenko et al. (2025)

Start with one change

You do not need to overhaul your life overnight. Pick one column that fits your current lifestyle, then add another when it sticks.

Reduce consumption

  • Choose reusables for drinks, bags, and food containers
  • Buy in bulk where it cuts packaging per serving
  • Skip single-use cutlery, straws, and mini toiletries

Sustainable choices

  • Favour natural fibres to cut microfibre shedding from laundry
  • Use glass or metal for storage and cooking when practical
  • Support brands that publish packaging and reduction targets

Spread awareness

  • Share reliable sources with friends and colleagues
  • Join clean-ups or local plastic-reduction initiatives
  • Vote with your wallet and ask retailers for better options
